# Applying Zero Trust to the Bangladesh Bank Heist

---

## Objective

Analyze the Bangladesh Bank Heist (2016) through the lens of Zero Trust Architecture and propose security improvements that could have prevented or mitigated the attack.

---

## Background

In 2016, attackers compromised systems at Bangladesh Bank and used stolen credentials to initiate fraudulent transactions via the SWIFT network, resulting in the theft of **$81 million**. The attackers exploited a lack of internal security controls, weak monitoring, and implicit trust between systems.

---

## Tasks

### Task 1 — Incident Analysis

- Summarize what occurred during the Bangladesh Bank Heist and how the attackers successfully executed the breach.
- Identify **2–3 major security weaknesses** that contributed to the incident.

---

### Task 2 — Zero Trust Recommendations

Recommend **at least three** Zero Trust-based improvements that could have prevented or reduced the impact of the attack.

For each recommendation, address the following:

- Which **core Zero Trust principle** does it support?
- What **specific tools or techniques** could be used to implement the control?

---

### Task 3 — High-Level Architecture Diagram *(Optional but Encouraged)*

Provide a simple diagram illustrating your proposed Zero Trust architecture for a secure financial system.
